I highly recommend the book What I Talk About When I Talk About Running by Haruki Murakami. It is not just a memoir about Murakami's experience as a runner; it was an absolute joy to read it. I like his philosophy on life, creativity, and the power of discipline.

Some quotes in this book I like:

Pain is inevitable. Suffering is optional.

When it comes to other people, you can always come up with a reasonable explanation, but you can't fool yourself.

I've always done whatever I felt like doing in life. People may try to stop me, and convince me I'm wrong, but I won't change.

It doesn’t matter how old I get, but as long as I continue to live I’ll always discover something new about myself.
